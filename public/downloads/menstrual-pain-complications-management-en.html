Systemic Effects of Prostaglandins

Prostaglandins not only affect the uterus but also impact multiple body systems:

üí° Multi-System Effects of Prostaglandins

  • Digestive System: Causes gastrointestinal smooth muscle contractions, leading to nausea, vomiting, diarrhea
  • Vascular System: Causes vasoconstriction and vasodilation, leading to headaches, fatigue
  • Nervous System: Triggers referred pain through neural reflexes, such as back pain
  • Water-Salt Metabolism: Works with hormones to cause sodium and water retention, leading to bloating

üéà Menstrual Bloating: More Than Just "Overeating"

Multiple Causes of Bloating

1. Hormonal Bloating

  • Estrogen Fluctuations: Affects intestinal motility, slowing digestion
  • Progesterone Effects: Relaxes smooth muscles, including intestinal muscles
  • Water-Sodium Retention: Hormonal changes cause fluid retention

2. Prostaglandin-Induced Bloating

  • Intestinal Inflammation: Prostaglandins cause mild inflammatory response in intestines
  • Intestinal Spasms: Irregular intestinal contractions
  • Gas Accumulation: Digestive dysfunction leads to increased gas production

ü§¢ Menstrual Nausea and Vomiting: Body's Protective Response

Physiological Mechanism of Nausea

üß† Nausea Center Activation Pathways

  1. Prostaglandin Stimulation: Direct action on the vomiting center in the medulla
  2. Pain Reflex: Severe pain triggers nausea through neural reflexes
  3. Hormonal Influence: Estrogen and progesterone affect gastrointestinal function
  4. Blood Sugar Fluctuations: Menstrual metabolic changes affect blood sugar stability

Menstrual Nausea Relief Guide

Immediate Relief Methods

  • Ginger Therapy: Ginger tea, ginger candy, use every 2-3 hours
  • Acupressure: Neiguan point (2 inches above wrist crease), press for 3-5 minutes
  • Peppermint Therapy: Peppermint tea or peppermint oil inhalation
  • Deep Breathing: Slow deep breathing, 10-15 times each session

Preventive Measures

  • Small Frequent Meals: Avoid empty stomach, eat small amounts every 2-3 hours
  • Avoid Irritants: Reduce greasy, spicy, overly sweet foods
  • Adequate Rest: Ensure 7-8 hours of sleep
  • Stress Management: Practice meditation, yoga, and other relaxation techniques

üîô Menstrual Back Pain: Complex Mechanism of Referred Pain

Anatomical Basis of Back Pain

Neural Anatomical Connections

  • Sympathetic Nerves: Uterus and lumbosacral region share T10-L1 nerve segments
  • Parasympathetic Nerves: Sacral nerves S2-S4 innervate both uterus and lumbosacral region
  • Referred Pain Mechanism: Uterine pain transmitted to lower back through spinal cord

Musculoskeletal Factors

  • Postural Changes: Pain leads to protective postures, increasing lower back burden
  • Muscle Tension: Abdominal pain causes compensatory tension in back muscles
  • Ligament Laxity: Hormones affect ligament stability
